A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

"Missile Man of India"

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. He was born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India on October 15, 1931. He was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work in the development of India's missile program. He was also a prolific writer and speaker, and he was known for his inspirational quotes.


Kalam was a brilliant student and he excelled in mathematics and science. He graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology with a degree in aeronautical engineering. Kalam was also a strong advocate for education and scientific progress. He was also a firm believer in the importance of peace and harmony. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015. He is remembered as a great leader and a visionary who inspired millions of Indians.


◆ Early Life and Education

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. His father was a boatman and his mother was a homemaker. Kalam had seven siblings. He was a bright student and he excelled in mathematics and science. Kalam graduated from the Madras Institute of Technology with a degree in aeronautical engineering in 1954. He then went on to work for the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).


◆ Career

Kalam played a key role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, the SLV-III. He also worked on the development of the Agni missile, which is India's first long-range ballistic missile. Kalam was also involved in the development of India's nuclear program. He was the scientific advisor to the defense minister during India's nuclear tests in 1998.


◆ Presidency

Kalam was elected President of India in 2002. He served as President for five years. During his time as President, Kalam worked to improve education and healthcare in India. He also visited many countries around the world to promote peace and understanding.


◆ Awards and Recognition

Kalam received numerous awards and honors, including the Padma Bhushan in 1981, the Padma Vibhushan in 1990, and the Bharat Ratna in 1997. He was also nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize in 2011.


◆ Legacy

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam is remembered as a great leader and a visionary who inspired millions of Indians. He was a strong advocate for education and scientific progress. He also believed in the importance of peace and harmony. Kalam's legacy will continue to inspire generations of Indians.
